A few house notes

Make yourself at home.

We want you to relax and enjoy the place. A handful of these matter for safety and the home’s well-and-septic systems — the rest are just the good habits that keep the chalet lovely for you and the guests who follow. Thanks for treating it like your own.

Good to know about the house

Well & septic

The home runs on a private well and septic system, so please flush only toilet paper — nothing else — to keep everything running smoothly. A faint mineral smell or slight discoloration in the water is completely normal up here.

Hot tub

For everyone’s safety, please keep glassware away from the tub and an eye on little ones, it’s best for ages 13 and up. Pop the cover back on when you’re done so it stays warm and clean. Well water can cause slight discoloration and a stray leaf sometimes drifts in; just give us a heads-up if anything looks off when you arrive.

Fireplace

It’s an easy-to-use gas fireplace, just please don’t place anything inside it.

Smoking

The chalet is a smoke and vape-free home. You’re welcome to step outside (just tidy up after) that way we both avoid the deep clean that indoor smoking unfortunately requires ($500).

Firearms

For the comfort of all our guests, we ask that firearms stay at home.

The little things

Check-in & check-out

Check-in is 3 PM and check-out is 11 AM. Need a little flexibility? Just ask. We’ll always try, though it depends on that day’s turnover.

Pets

We love animals! Pets are welcome with a little advance notice (and certified service animals always, of course). We just ask that they stay off the beds, furniture, and carpet, aren’t left alone unless crated, and that you tidy up after them.

The grill

If you fire it up, a quick clean and the cover back on keeps it ready for the next cookout. It takes a winter break (Nov 1 – Apr 15) to stay protected from the elements.

The neighborhood

A few community heads-ups.

Towamensing Trails is a close-knit place — about 40% of folks live here year-round, and the community keeps a friendly eye on things. Here’s the lay of the land so none of the HOA’s rules catch you by surprise.

Quiet hours, 10 PM – 7 AM

Sound carries in the woods and security does patrol overnight, so please keep things mellow after 10pm, especially out by the hot tub. Late-night noise can draw a fine ($500) from the community, and we’d hate for that to land on your trip.

The wildlife

You may spot deer and, now and then, a bear. Please don’t feed them. This is their backyard too, and a fed animal tends to keep coming back.

Parking passes

Every car in the driveway needs an HOA pass. Your stay includes 6, with a couple more available at $25 each. Street parking isn’t allowed (and they do tow), so let’s keep everyone in the driveway.

Take it slow on the roads

There are lots of kids and animals around, and community security tickets speeders directly. Easy does it and you’ll be all set.

No open fires, fireworks, hunting, or firearms

The community doesn’t permit any of these — including paintball guns, BB guns, and bows — and they’re strict about it, especially the open-fire ban in dry weather.

Unlicensed vehicles

ATVs, scooters, pocket bikes, and snowmobiles aren’t allowed in the development.

Amenity passes

You’ll want a wristband for the beach, lake, pool, clubhouse, and courts, up to 8 per group. Kids under 12 should have an adult along, and these amenities are open in the summer season.

Bringing a dog?

Keep pups leashed and clean up after them anywhere in the development. The beach is the one spot that’s off-limits to dogs.

Be prepared

Power outages.

Outages happen occasionally up here. The breaker panel is in the 1st-floor hallway by the entrance; flashlights and batteries are in a bin in the master bathroom closet. If a reset doesn’t help, contact Steve (714)925-0819.

After an outage: turn off all switches and power right away and clear the stovetop and oven, so there’s no fire risk when power returns. Candles aren’t permitted except during an emergency outage.
